ENFP Personality Webpage

Interpretation

The best way I can describe the campaigner personality type is as the big dreamers that can never settle. Our curiosity and enthusiasm is always driving us forward, which can unfortunately lead to very distracted and overthinking minds. We are very people focused and this generally results in an appreciation of networking and seeing the world as one large connected organism. This often means developed communication skills and an altruistic mindset, which in turn leads to inspiring and influential social characters. The world is infinitely large and we endeavour to understand as much of it as we can, especially when it comes to understanding people and what makes us tick. With these considerations, we are natural mentors who like to question everything.

Unfortunately for us, this also leads to our biggest weakness which is the implementation and execution of our ideas in a practical manner. Being too focused on discussing ideas can detract from time spent realising them, not to mention becoming easily distracted by other ideas and overcommitting to other people and projects. This promulgates into an absolute contempt towards being micromanaged or restricted, an easy source of heightened emotional expression and stress. We generally know what is being asked of us, we just need the freedom to accomplish it rather than having a superior or colleague riding our grill to get it done.

Teamwork

The ENFP personality will generally thrive in a team environment. We are creative and flexible and can understand alternate perspectives effectively to ensure cohesion where others may generate confusion due to communication gaps. We also are generally good motivators as we gain infectious excitement, believing in the intrinsic motivation of doing things for the enjoyment, potential and curiosity of simply doing it. The key thing to watch out for here is that this excitement can often lead to us derailing from the subject at hand and potentially wasting time that could otherwise be spent more productively. It is in these instances where someone with higher levels of discipline and organisational skills can assist in keeping things on track by being assertive when necessary. While other personalities may see collaboration as a pyramid hierarchy, my personality type frequently looks at collaboration as more of a mesh network with everyone talking together through the most direct and effective routes to optimize efficiency and help ensure everyone remains on the same page. This can go both ways when interacting with other personalities depending on how those in the team feel, some may appreciate the level of open dialogue and updates, however others may feel this inappropriate, irritating or too intrusive, which can lead to stepping on others toes unintentionally and unnecessary group turmoil. Finally, while people with my personality type can be great at motivating, organising, analysing and generating ideas, our poor practical skills can often mean poor timeliness and procrastination for more mundane tasks like writing up the final reports or compiling all the documentation. When it comes to teamwork, having someone working alongside who is happy to assist with this, or even just motivating the ENFP personality to get it over and done with for the feeling of accomplishment, can be a significant help towards meeting deadlines and accomplishing the big ideas we like to find.

Project Ideas

Overview

As an avid Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition (D&D 5e) player and aspiring Dungeon Master (DM) which is the story teller and world builder who referees the game rules and adds narrative flare to the mechanics of the game for the other players, my project idea is in support of this. A simple standalone Critical Cards application with a very generic user interface and checkboxes designed to meet the circumstances of the players. Once the appropriate options are selected, they “draw a card” to determine the outcome electronically, thus reducing clutter at the table and improving convenience. This is not a part of the official rules of the game, but rather in support of them, giving greater consequences to the successes and failures of the players to enhance the experience for all the players.


Motivation

I am extremely invested in D&D 5e with years of playing the tabletop with my friends in various regular groups. There already exist many IT projects which support or compliment the gameplay of D&D 5e, including Roll20, D&D Beyond, The Homebrewery, Steam workshop extensions to Tabletop Simulator and a variety of character sheet generators and dice rollers. This has led many players to bringing laptops and phones to the gaming table for the convenience over pen and paper. A common highlight of D&D 5e is critical successes and critical fails, when despite the odds, the dice either demand your achievements or doom your attempts. While cards and other homebrew rules have been made where you roll against tables to determine the detailed outcomes and flavour of these rolls, there doesn’t appear to be a simple GUI application that enables these to be quickly accessed by the players. With so many players and DMs turning to IT to enhance their gameplay, experienced players will certainly appreciate the convenience of an easy to use and easy to access program.


Details

Within D&D 5e, a 20-sided dice is frequently used to determine outcomes of a situation, adding modifiers to the roll based on the characters strengths and weaknesses. As an extension to this, players enjoy adding additional rules for when a 1 (fail) or a 20 (success) are rolled on the dice, referring to them as criticals. This is most often occurring in combat, but can sometimes also occur outside of combat in roleplayed situations, whether it be interacting with Non-Player Characters (NPCs), overcoming traps, solving puzzles or just general world exploration. The project idea acknowledges these circumstances and sets up a series of checkboxes and radio buttons to reflect the situation and level progression of the Player Characters (PCs), so whenever they roll a 20 or a 1 in any given circumstance, they randomly determine further benefits/consequences.

The overall project in its initial plan is aimed at being finalised as a standalone executable file for PCs, however further developments could be made to translate it into a webapp or even a mobile app for usage on phones without requiring internet access. The title would be listed as D&D 5e Crit Cards. Underneath this would exist 4 tabs which identify the 4 different types of decks that can be drawn from; Critical Hits for Players, Critical Hits for DMs, Luck Cards and Critical Misses (Fumble). At the bottom of all these tabs, will exist a centred button with the text DRAW. Beneath this will be a text window which includes a title of the effect and a short description of what occurs. This text will change whenever the draw button is pressed and will randomly draw from the pool of options depending on what is selected by the checkboxes and radio buttons which vary based on the tab selected.

Within the Players tab, will exist two rows. The first row is 4 checkboxes to select what severity will be included within the draw and a recommended level range. These are Setback (from level 1), Dangerous (from level 5), Life Threatening (from level 9) and Deadly (from level 13). The second row beneath this will be a series of radio buttons indicating the damage types of Slashing, Piercing, Bludgeoning or Magic. Beneath these two rows will exist standardised button and text. The DMs tab would have the same layout as the Players tab, however would pull from a different pool of cards balanced specifically for the DM to use when the NPCs critically strike against players.

The Luck tab would simply have a radio button for Good Luck or Bad Luck. These are for circumstances outside of combat when a player rolls a 1 or a 20. The player keeps a good luck card until they wish to use it, a player keeps a bad luck card until the DM chooses to use it against them. Updated iterations of the project may include the option to input a PCs Name and preserve the good and bad luck cards they draw, enabling players to select the cards they’ve been allocated and revealing the card description within the text box as if they had clicked the draw button.

The Fumble tab would include 3 checkboxes and 4 radio buttons in a similar layout to the two critical hit tabs for Players and DMs. The check boxes denote the nature of the fail to be included within the draw; Embarrassing, Awkward and Shameful. The radio buttons reflect the nature of the attacks; Melee, Ranged, Natural, Magic. As per the other tabs, clicking the draw button will fill/change the text with a randomly selected effect chosen from the pools as determined by the selected options.


Tools and Tech

This project can be achieved with using Python. It may not be the most optimal, but it would certainly be an entertaining approach and a good test of the skills developed in the parallel Introduction To Programming course most students of this course are currently completing. The user interface would be achieved through the use of the Tk interface otherwise known as the tkinter package. In order to achieve a single executable file, pyinstaller would be used to package the libraries and dependencies together with the project code. The component that poses the most difficulty would be generating the cards texts and associated tags and linking them to be pulled by a random module. The easiest path forward would be using an SQL database, however a clever use of dictionary keys attributed to nested dictionaries and lists of variables could achieve a similar outcome although much less efficiently and far more messy.


Skills Required

For this to be project to be achieved, the most significant requirement would be motivation backed by a basic grasp of programming and working with python. Considering the tables and cards for the D&D 5e Critical outcomes already exist, there isn’t particularly a requirement for a comprehensive knowledge of D&D, only a basic understanding of what is being asked of the project. While it would be useful to have prior experience or skill with tkinter, pyinstaller and SQL databases, motivated programmers should be more than capable of independent research into these to complement their Python skills and achieve the minimum requirements of the project.


Outcome

On completion of the project, the development will help ensure that these optional rules will be more readily available for D&D 5e groups to implement into their games. As with many IT solutions, rather than reinventing the wheel, it is simply offering a more convenient version of already existing solutions. Any D&D player can write up a list of events that occur and number them, rolling a dice to see what number is matched, or writing up cards and shuffling them to draw. Maintaining such tables, dice and cards can be cumbersome and perhaps a player may not have these handy at the time or simply forgot to bring them to a session. This project would ensure that everyone has access to such things digitally. Perhaps this could even become the foundation for development into a larger and more flexible tool that easily allows others to create their own tables of chance used to enhance their gameplay experience.
